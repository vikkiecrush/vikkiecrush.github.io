Introduction

Before we can lead in AI/ML, we have to understand where AI came from. This artifact is a research-based visual and written timeline tracing the evolution of Artificial Intelligence across eight decades — from theoretical foundations, through cycles of hype and disappointment, to the deep learning revolution and today's foundation-model era.

Description

The artifact consists of two paired deliverables:

  • Visual timeline (Canva): a designed graphical timeline plotting the major AI milestones from 1943 to present.
  • Written narrative: a four-paragraph analysis contextualizing the key events, the AI Winters, the deep-learning breakthrough, and the current era of competitive foundation-model development.

Key milestones covered include: McCulloch & Pitts (1943), Turing's imitation game (1950), the Dartmouth Workshop (1956), the First and Second AI Winters (1974-1980, 1987-1993), AlexNet + GPUs (2012), and the modern era dominated by OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Meta, and Anthropic.

Objective

To develop a foundational understanding of AI history that grounds all future coursework and professional AI/ML decisions. The premise: emerging AI leaders need historical context to distinguish genuine breakthroughs from hype cycles — because we have been here before, and we know what happens when expectations outrun capability.

Process

  1. 1
    Research — reviewed the assigned course materials and cross-referenced with public sources (Our World in Data, IEEE Computer Society, third-party historical retrospectives).
  2. 2
    Organize — identified major eras (Foundations, First Winter, Expert Systems, Second Winter, ML/Deep Learning, Modern Era) and the milestone events that define each.
  3. 3
    Draft narrative — wrote a four-paragraph analytical summary tracing the arc: theory → setback → breakthrough → commercial competition.
  4. 4
    Design visual — used Canva to produce a colorful, modern timeline graphic that communicates the same content visually for a general professional audience.
  5. 5
    AI-assisted editing — used ChatGPT to help organize the timeline structure and refine the wording; verified all facts independently against the primary course materials. Included an explicit AI Assistance Disclosure with the deliverable.

Value Proposition

“This timeline gives any AI/ML professional the historical context needed to distinguish genuine paradigm shifts from hype cycles. It equips me — and anyone who reads it — to make sober judgments about AI investments, capabilities, and limitations in the current era.”

Unique Value

Many AI history explainers focus on the breakthroughs and skip the AI Winters. This artifact intentionally highlights both, because the pattern of hype followed by disillusionment is the most important lesson for any leader entering AI/ML today. Understanding why funding collapsed in 1974 and again in 1987 is what separates a thoughtful AI leader from someone chasing the next trend.

The artifact also models responsible AI-assisted work: I used ChatGPT transparently, verified every fact against primary sources, and disclosed the assistance publicly. That transparency itself is an artifact of the ethical posture I bring to AI/ML.

Relevance

For organizations considering AI investments, for teams evaluating vendor claims, and for leaders shaping AI strategy: this timeline is a reference tool. It demonstrates my ability to synthesize dense technical history into a communicable narrative — a critical skill for anyone who will need to explain AI concepts to non-technical executives, board members, or cross-functional stakeholders.
