ARTIFACT 01 Custom GPT Design Thinking Ethical AI

Nil Pick Detective

A Socratic coaching bot that guides new grocery-fulfillment supervisors through a structured, evidence-based root-cause investigation of nil-pick incidents — without ever assigning blame.

Try the Live Bot →

Introduction

“Nil Pick Detective” is a Custom GPT built to solve a real operational problem I encountered in grocery e-commerce fulfillment: newly promoted supervisors receive daily nil-pick reports (instances where an ordered item cannot be found) but have no structured method to investigate the root causes. They guess, they blame pickers, or they ignore the report entirely. The bot serves as a private, judgment-free thinking partner that walks them through a rigorous, Socratic investigation in under five minutes.

Description

The bot offers two conversation modes:

  • Mode A — Diagnose an incident: walks the user through a five-branch decision tree (Availability → Findability → Identifiability → Process → Data), one branch at a time.
  • Mode B — 5 Whys deep dive: iterative drilling for recurring patterns, always steering toward systemic root causes rather than individual blame.

It is bound by explicit rules that require it to (a) frame every cause as a HYPOTHESIS, not a verdict, (b) lead with systemic explanations before considering individual behavior, (c) refuse to accept confidential company data even when volunteered, and (d) close each investigation with a testable experiment rather than a conclusion.

Objective

To design a Custom GPT that teaches a repeatable, ethical investigation method — not just to answer questions, but to build diagnostic thinking skills in new supervisors so they can apply the same framework independently after the conversation ends. Success means the user walks away with a hypothesis and a testable experiment, not with someone to blame.

Process

I applied the full Design Thinking methodology across five phases:

  1. 1
    Empathy — profiled the target user (“Sam, the New Ops Supervisor”): overwhelmed, nervous, wants a private thinking partner without judgment.
  2. 2
    Define — scoped the problem narrowly: guide root-cause investigation using only public retail research, no company data, no personnel evaluation.
  3. 3
    Ideate — brainstormed five candidate modes; selected two (Socratic diagnosis + 5 Whys) for v1 and deferred three others.
  4. 4
    Prototype — wrote the full Instructions block covering role, user, core behaviors, decision tree, knowledge boundaries, ethics, and conversation close, then deployed as a live Custom GPT.
  5. 5
    Test — ran 8 tests across happy path, adversarial (bias probe, data leak, hallucination), and edge cases. v1 scored 4/8. Identified structural gaps in the KNOWLEDGE BOUNDARIES rule, rewrote it into three components (detection + response template + echo-suppression), and re-tested. v2 scored 6/6 with zero data leaks.

Value Proposition

“Nil Pick Detective proves that thoughtful AI design — not raw model power — is what makes an AI product usable, ethical, and durable. It demonstrates that with the right structural rules, an LLM can teach diagnostic thinking, protect confidential data, and preserve human dignity all in the same conversation.”

Unique Value

Most retail AI tools optimize outcomes (recommend a substitution, predict stockouts, route the pick path). This bot optimizes reasoning — it teaches the user how to think, not just what to conclude. That is a rare category.

The v2 test cycle also produced a discovery worth noting: with the right rule structure, aligned behavior can emerge without being explicitly programmed. Under user pressure to skip questioning, the bot proactively defended the picker (ethics rule) and framed its response as a working theory (hypothesis rule) — behaviors that were never coded together but emerged from the rule stack interacting. This is the kind of AI product design that inspires confidence.

Relevance

For any recruiter, hiring manager, or organization evaluating AI/ML talent: this artifact demonstrates end-to-end AI product thinking. Not just prompt engineering, but a full cycle of user empathy, structural rule design, adversarial testing, version iteration, and honest reflection on both successes and failures. That combination — technical fluency, product judgment, and ethical vigilance — is exactly what organizations need as they move from AI experimentation to responsible AI deployment.
